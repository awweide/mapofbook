## Main infoboxes

**Summary**
Prince Raoden of Arelon awakens one morning to find that the Shaod has taken him, transforming him into an undying, pain‑wracked husk. Thrown into the decaying city of Elantris, he refuses despair and secretly builds a community among the cursed, searching for the lost magic of AonDor. Meanwhile, his betrothed, Princess Sarene of Teod, arrives to discover she is a widow by contract, bound to a dead husband. Thrust into the treacherous Arelene court, she battles the schemes of the Fjordell gyorn Hrathen, who has only three months to convert the kingdom to Shu‑Dereth—or see it annihilated. As Sarene’s Widow’s Trial threatens Elantris’s fragile hope, Raoden unravels the Aons’ connection to the land. When the two worlds collide, Raoden completes the final line and restores the city’s power, defeating the brutal Dakhor monks and securing a future for both Arelon and Teod.

**Author**
**Brandon Sanderson** (born 1975) is an American epic fantasy author, best known for the Mistborn series, the Stormlight Archive, and for completing Robert Jordan’s The Wheel of Time. Renowned for intricate magic systems and expansive world‑building, he debuted with *Elantris* (2005), the first published work set in his shared Cosmere universe. He teaches creative writing at Brigham Young University.

**Importance**
*Elantris* launched Sanderson’s career and introduced his signature blend of rigorous magic, political intrigue, and character‑driven storytelling. A cornerstone of the Cosmere, it establishes core concepts—the Dor, AonDor, Investiture—that recur throughout his later works. Beloved by fans, it remains a prime entry point to modern epic fantasy.

**Themes**
- The power of hope and purpose in the face of overwhelming despair  
- The corruption of rigid honour codes and institutional religion  
- Identity, transformation, and what makes a person human  
- Political manipulation, leadership, and the cost of power  
- Love transcending physical form and social expectation  
- The deep connection between language, magic, and geography

**Message**
Sanderson argues that true strength lies in the refusal to surrender to suffering. Raoden’s revolution in Elantris and Sarene’s defiance of political norms show that even broken systems can be remade by individuals who choose hope. The novel also critiques the use of faith as a tool of control, suggesting that genuine belief cannot be imposed from above.

## Structure

*Elantris* is narrated in the past tense, using third‑person limited perspective that rotates rigidly through three viewpoint characters: **Raoden**, **Sarene**, and **Hrathen**. Each chapter is headed by the name of the character whose consciousness the narrator follows, and the chapters appear in a strict repeating triad (Raoden – Sarene – Hrathen). This pattern is maintained almost without exception from Chapter 1 through Chapter 62, creating a balanced, braided narrative where three simultaneous plots advance in lockstep.

The only significant departure occurs in the **Epilogue**, which shifts to an omniscient perspective and concludes with Sarene’s public eulogy for Hrathen.

The novel is organised into three named parts—*The Shadow of Elantris*, *The Call of Elantris*, and *The Spirit of Elantris*—each ending with a major climax: Hrathen’s exile, Telrii’s death and Sarene’s apparent fall to the Shaod, and finally the restoration of the city and the defeat of Dilaf. Time flows chronologically with no extended analepsis or prolepsis, though brief flashbacks appear in the form of dreams, memories, and quoted letters; the main action spans roughly three months. The tight triadic structure is one of the novel’s most distinctive formal features, reflecting its thematic insistence on the interplay between hope (Raoden), action (Sarene), and ideology (Hrathen).

## Phases

| Phase | Brief Description |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Phase 1: The Shadow of Elantris (Chapters 1–27)** | Raoden is taken by the Shaod and thrown into Elantris, where he begins to organise the desperate inhabitants and searches for the secret of AonDor. Sarene arrives in Arelon, learns her betrothed is “dead”, and immerses herself in court politics to oppose Hrathen’s Derethi conversion campaign. Hrathen seeks to unify the nobility behind fear of Elantris while maneuvering to place a sympathetic ruler on the throne. The phase ends with Hrathen’s self‑poisoning and exile into Elantris—a gambit to prove Jaddeth’s power. |
| **Phase 2: The Call of Elantris (Chapters 28–54)** | Sarene’s Widow’s Trial brings food into Elantris, complicating Raoden’s efforts to make his people self‑sufficient. Raoden and Galladon discover the Aons’ link to Arelon’s geography and the crucial Chasm line. Hrathen’s plan to depose Iadon succeeds, but the new king Telrii proves an unreliable ally. Sarene is artificially “cursed” by the Shaod with a Fjordell poison, and inside Elantris she finally meets Raoden, though she does not recognise him. Raoden perfects an illusion Aon and escapes to Kae as “Kaloo”, working with the nobles to oppose Telrii and the fanatical Dilaf. The phase culminates in Telrii’s assassination, the revelation of Raoden’s true nature, and Dilaf’s devastating surprise attack. |
| **Phase 3: The Spirit of Elantris (Chapters 55–Epilogue)** | Dilaf’s Dakhor monks massacre New Elantris and capture Sarene. Raoden, broken and nearly delivered to the pool of dissolution, has a final epiphany: Aon Rao is a map of the five cities. He completes the missing Chasm line, restoring Elantris’s power and his own transformation. The Elantrians emerge from the flames and drive out the Fjordell forces. Hrathen, mortally wounded, sacrifices himself to kill Dilaf and save Sarene. Raoden and Sarene marry, and Arelon begins to rebuild under a just monarchy. |

## Foreshadowing in *Elantris*

| Title | Description | Appearances |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| **The Chasm Line** | Throughout the early chapters, Raoden’s Aons flash and fail without effect. The explanation—that the Aons are maps requiring a new line for the Chasm generated by the Reod—is subtly hinted at by the constant emphasis on geography and the earthquake. | 7, 16, 19, 25, 35, 43, 55, 61 |
| **The Dor as a Living Force** | The Dor is described as a pressure seeking release. Raoden’s unusually violent Dor attacks foreshadow that his body is a potential conduit, not merely a victim; the final restoration occurs when he becomes the focus for the entire city. | 16, 19, 25, 31, 37, 43, 55, 61 |
| **The Seon Madness** | The mad seons floating in Elantris, including Raoden’s Ien, are early clues that the Shaod affects not just bodies but also their bond to Investiture. Their eventual restoration parallels the healing of Elantris. | 7, 12, 13, 16, 18, 43 |
| **Raoden’s Identity** | Spirit’s educated speech, his knowledge of barons, and his determination to help Elantris are constant hints that he is more than a minor noble. The revelation that he is Prince Raoden is heavily foreshadowed. | 1–7, 13, 22, 28, 40–44, 52–53 |
| **Hrathen’s Arm** | Hrathen’s constant wearing of a cloth sleeve on his right arm, the memory of Dakhor training, and his unusual strength hint at his hidden bone patterns. The arm ultimately becomes the means to kill Dilaf. | 3, 6, 18, 21, 36, 58, 60–61 |
| **The Pool of Dissolution** | Galladon and Raoden discover a pool on the mountain where the Hoed dissolve peacefully. Raoden makes Galladon swear to bring him there if he falls, foreshadowing his near‑fate in Chapter 61. | 31, 59, 61 |
| **Aons as Maps** | Sarene, during an AonDor lesson, offhandedly remarks that every Aon begins with a drawing that resembles Arelon. This casual observation becomes the key to restoring AonDor. | 7, 35, 43, 46, 55, 61 |
| **The Mad Seons** – The aimless, mad seons that float through Elantris are an eerie, ghostly presence that unsettles everyone and foreshadow the fractured connection to the Dor that will later be mended. | 7, 12, 13, 16, 18, 43, 61 |
| **Dilaf’s True Nature** | Dilaf’s intense hatred of Elantris, his unnatural speed, and the suspicious scars beneath his skin hint that he is something far more dangerous than a simple arteth. His final transformation reveals the bone carvings of Dakhor. | 6, 9, 18, 25, 30, 42, 51, 56–58 |
| **The Importance of Food** | The initial focus on the Elantrians’ hunger—and Raoden’s decision to stockpile seed corn rather than eat it—foreshadows that self‑sufficient agriculture will be the basis of New Elantris’s survival, independent of outside charity. | 4, 10, 13, 22, 28, 34, 40 |
| **Sarene’s Political Acumen** | Sarene’s early manipulation of Iadon with feigned stupidity, her skill at fencing, and her boldness in the council all foreshadow that she will be the one to hold the kingdom together when Raoden is incapacitated. | 5, 8, 11, 14, 20, 23, 29, 39, 49 |

## Glossary

### Characters

**Raoden**  
*Representations:* Prince Raoden, Spirit, Lord Spirit, Kaloo, the Elantrian prince  
*Explanation:* Crown prince of Arelon. Taken by the Shaod, he leads the Elantrians as “Spirit” and later restores the city’s magic. He is kind, brilliant, and determined.  
*See also:* Sarene, Galladon, Karata, Saolin, Ien  
*Appearances:* 1–63, Epilogue

**Sarene**  
*Representations:* Princess Sarene, Lady Sarene, Your Highness, the Teo princess, Sarene  
*Explanation:* Daughter of King Eventeo of Teod. Married by contract to Raoden, then widowed, she uses her political skill to defend Arelon from Fjorden’s influence and falls in love with Spirit.  
*See also:* Raoden, Eventeo, Kiin, Ashe  
*Appearances:* 2–63, Epilogue

**Hrathen**  
*Representations:* Gyorn Hrathen, High Priest Hrathen, the gyorn, Lord Hrathen  
*Explanation:* A high priest of Shu‑Dereth sent to convert Arelon within three months. He later questions his mission and sacrifices himself to save Sarene and stop Dilaf.  
*See also:* Dilaf, Wyrn, Forton, Eventeo

### Concepts and Magical Terms

**AonDor**  
*Representations:* AonDor, the magic of the Aons, Elantrian magic  
*Explanation:* The system of drawing precise Aons in the air to produce magical effects; usable only by Elantrians.  
*Appearances:* 7, 16, 19, 25, 35, 43, 49, 55, 61

**Aons**  
*Representations:* Aons, the characters  
*Explanation:* Complex symbols that are both the letters of the Aonic alphabet and the focus for magical power. Each Aon is a map of Arelon with specific modifications.  
*Appearances:* 7, 16, 19, 25, 35, 43, 55

**The Dor**  
*Representations:* the Dor, the overspirit, the power  
*Explanation:* A vast, pressurised, non‑sentient energy source that fuels AonDor. It seeks any release, including through faulty or incomplete Aons.  
*Appearances:* 16, 19, 25, 35, 37, 43

**The Shaod**  
*Representations:* the Shaod, the Transformation, the curse  
*Explanation:* The random, night‑time event that transforms an ordinary person into an Elantrian. Since the Reod, it creates cursed, pain‑afflicted forms.  
*Appearances:* 1, 4, 7, 16, 25, 33, 39

**The Reod**  
*Representations:* the Reod, the Punishment, the Fall  
*Explanation:* The cataclysmic earthquake ten years prior that fractured the land, broke AonDor, and turned Elantris into a prison of pain.  
*Appearances:* Prologue, 1, 7, 25, 35

**Seons**  
*Representations:* seons, the glowing balls  
*Explanation:* Sentient spheres of light with an Aon at their core; they bond to a master and serve as companions and communicators. They go mad if their master is taken by the Shaod.  
*Appearances:* 1, 5, 7, 12–13, 16, 18, 24, 43, 63

**Svrakiss**  
*Representations:* Svrakiss, demons, devils  
*Explanation:* In Derethi theology, the souls of evil men who oppose Jaddeth. Hrathen convinces the populace that Elantrians are Svrakiss.  
*Appearances:* 6, 30

**Shu‑Dereth**  
*Representations:* Shu‑Dereth, the Derethi faith, the Fjordell religion  
*Explanation:* The militaristic, hierarchal religion of Fjorden that seeks to unite all mankind under Wyrn’s rule.  
*Appearances:* 3, 6, 9, 12, 33, 36, 45

**Shu‑Korath**  
*Representations:* Shu‑Korath, the Korathi faith, Domi’s church  
*Explanation:* The gentler, love‑focused religion dominant in Arelon and Teod. Its symbol is Aon Omi.  
*Appearances:* 2, 5, 14, 24, 30, 38

**Jeskeri Mysteries**  
*Representations:* the Jeskeri Mysteries, the Mysteries  
*Explanation:* A dark, syncretic cult involving ritual sacrifice, secretly practised by Iadon.  
*Appearances:* 33

**The Chasm**  
*Representations:* the Chasm, the crack, the earthquake rift  
*Explanation:* The enormous fissure that opened in southern Arelon during the Reod; its existence required a new line in every Aon for the magic to function.  
*Appearances:* 35, 43, 55, 61

**Hoed**  
*Representations:* the Hoed, the fallen, the mindless  
*Explanation:* Elantrians who have succumbed to their accumulated pain and lost all volition. They repeat mantras and are cared for by Raoden’s community.  
*Appearances:* 1, 4, 10, 25, 28, 31, 59, 61

**The AonDor Book**  
*Representations:* the AonDor book, the tome  
*Explanation:* The ancient manual on AonDor that Raoden uses to learn the magic, including illusion and healing Aons.  
*Appearances:* 16, 25, 35, 43, 55

**Forton’s Potion**  
*Representations:* the Shaod‑mimicking potion, Forton’s elixir  
*Explanation:* A concoction that temporarily makes the drinker appear as an Elantrian. Hrathen uses it on himself and later on Sarene.  
*Appearances:* 18, 21, 27, 39, 49

**“Kolo?”**  
*Representations:* Kolo?, kolo  
*Explanation:* A Duladen tag‑question meaning “isn’t that so?” or “right?” Galladon uses it constantly.  
*Appearances:* 1, 4, 6, 10, 13, 16, etc.

**“Sule”**  
*Representations:* sule  
*Explanation:* A Duladen term of endearment and friendship, used by Galladon toward Raoden.  
*Appearances:* 1, 4, 6, 10, 13, etc.

## Major Storylines

| Title | Description | Appearances |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| **Raoden’s Resurrection of Elantris** | Cursed by the Shaod, Prince Raoden is cast into the decaying city of Elantris, where he refuses to surrender to pain and despair. Under the alias “Spirit”, he organises the desperate inhabitants, builds the thriving community of New Elantris, and tirelessly studies AonDor. His quest to unlock the secret of the Reod drives the novel’s central magical arc, culminating in the restoration of Elantris and his own complete transformation. | 1, 4, 7, 10, 13, 16, 19, 22, 25, 28, 31, 34, 37, 40, 43, 46, 49, 52, 55, 58, 61, 63 |
| **Sarene’s Political Battle and Hidden Love** | Believing herself widowed, Princess Sarene throws herself into the treacherous politics of the Arelene court. She counters Hrathen’s conversion plans, befriends the rebellious nobility, organises a Widow’s Trial to demystify Elantris, and later must lead the kingdom after Iadon’s fall. Simultaneously, she develops a deep, complicated bond with the enigmatic “Spirit” inside Elantris, unaware he is her lost husband. Her storyline merges political intrigue with a slow‑burning romance. | 2, 5, 8, 11, 14, 17, 20, 23, 26, 29, 32, 35, 38, 41, 44, 47, 50, 53, 56, 59, 62, Epilogue |
| **Hrathen’s Mission and Crisis of Faith** | Gyorn Hrathen arrives in Kae with a divine deadline: convert Arelon to Shu‑Dereth in three months or watch it be destroyed. A master manipulator, he schemes to topple the monarchy, unify the nobility behind fear of Elantris, and stage a “miracle” to prove his god’s power. Yet as he wrestles with the fanatic Dilaf and his own hollow faith, Hrathen gradually realises he has been used as a distraction for a massacre. His arc ends with self‑sacrifice, saving the woman he secretly loves and the country he was sent to subjugate. | 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18, 21, 24, 27, 30, 33, 36, 39, 42, 45, 48, 51, 54, 57, 60 |
